# ENCePP AI Survey — Full Instrument

This document reproduces the complete survey instrument administered through the
ENCePP AI survey (version 6). The survey uses a branching design: a screening
question routes respondents to a pharmacovigilance (PV) or pharmacoepidemiology
(PE) track, with shared sections completed by all respondents.

**Definitions provided to respondents**

- **Artificial intelligence (AI):** a machine-based system that, for a given set
  of objectives, makes predictions, recommendations, or decisions influencing real
  or virtual environments (adapted from the OECD AI Principles and CIOMS).
- **AI tool:** software applications or programs that utilise artificial intelligence
  algorithms to perform tasks such as decision-making, problem-solving, and data
  analysis (ScienceDirect), adapted to the PE/PV context and including off-the-shelf
  systems (e.g., Microsoft Copilot, ChatGPT, Claude AI, Gemini), domain-specific
  platforms, and custom-built models.

## Section 4 — Infrastructure, governance, skills (shared; 19 items)

- **I-2** overall readiness
- **I-3, I-4** computing infrastructure type and adequacy
- **I-5, I-6, I-7, I-DS** data environment, dataset access, data barrier, data sources
- **I-8, I-9, I-10** software availability, technical support, validated tools
- **I-11, I-12, I-13, I-14** governance framework, EU AI Act readiness, model validation, regulatory awareness
- **I-15, I-16, I-17** expertise level, personal training, organisational training
- **I-18** terminology familiarity (ML, NLP, LLMs, predictive analytics, signal detection; 1–5)
- **I-1** single most critical barrier (synthesis question, placed at section end)

## Section 5 — Perceptions, attitudes, priorities (shared)

- **P-1** six Likert statements (1–5): GenAI impact · ethics · job security ·
  regulatory clarity · organisational readiness · trust in outputs
- **P-2** comfort implementing conversational AI (4-point)
- **P-3** enabling factors (up to 3)
- **P-4, P-5, P-6** open-ended: advice to leaders/regulators · training interest · further comments

## Section 6 — Optional AI tool inventory

Structured per-tool template with eight blocks (A–H): identification · scope &
application · data · performance & validation · outcomes & lessons · regulatory &
governance context · narrative account · availability & references. No mandatory fields.
